Wrapping Up the PRS Project with humess – 2024

In 2024, der BioIT joined forces with humess to tackle one of the most ambitious challenges in translational genomics: making polygenic risk scores (PRS) not only powerful, but usable — in real time, at scale, and without barriers.

Now, after a year of collaboration, development, and refinement, the project has successfully wrapped. And what we delivered together stands as a blueprint for what next-generation precision medicine can look like.

From the very beginning, our goal was clear: build a cloud-native, interactive PRS platform that could provide accurate risk estimates and instant visualizations for multiple diseases — all wrapped in an intuitive interface and backed by a robust API.

But we didn’t stop at the technical minimum.

Together with the humess team, we reimagined how PRS could be communicated to researchers, clinicians, and even patients. Our shared philosophy centered on transparency, flexibility, and modular design. That meant:

  • Incorporating population-specific summary statistics
  • Supporting multiple risk model formats (Bayesian, LDpred2, penalized regression)
  • Enabling real-time plotting and comparison of polygenic distributions
  • Ensuring GDPR-aware architecture for clinical deployment in the EU

Beyond the code, we worked shoulder-to-shoulder with the humess product and design teams to embed PRS into broader health intelligence workflows — making genetic insights a living part of decision-making, not a static PDF buried in a report.

The result? A dynamic, data-driven engine for predictive genomics at scale — powering web applications, informing digital health tools, and providing API access for ongoing integration into population studies and biobank research.

Ending the project with humess wasn’t just a milestone — it was a case study in how deep collaboration between bioinformatics and software product teams can move the field forward. Fast.

As we close this chapter, we’re proud of the system we built. But even more proud of the values it represents: open science, practical applications, and putting real genomic tools into the hands of people who can make a difference with them.

The PRS platform we built will continue to evolve under humess’ direction — and we’ll be cheering from the sidelines. Or jumping in again, if needed. Because that’s how good partnerships work.
